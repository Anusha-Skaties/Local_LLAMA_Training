# How do you determine the right model size vs cost trade-off?

Well, basically, finding the right model size is about deciding whether you need a giant, expensive brain for a hard task or a small, cheap brain for a simple one. It exists because the biggest models cost a lot of money and take a long time to think, which can ruin your budget and annoy your users. Unlike a basic chatbot project, a production system has a real bill at the end of the month that you need to justify. A simple way to think about it is like hiring a team where you don't need a senior engineer with a PhD just to sort some emails. One common beginner mistake is using the most powerful model available for every single task without testing if a smaller one could do it just as well.

## Real-world example

Notion uses a very smart model trade-off strategy for their AI writing features. They route simple tasks to smaller models and complex tasks to larger ones. This keeps performance fast and costs manageable.
